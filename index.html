About

I began my post-high school journey pursuing music education. An avid musician to this day, there was no question that I was going to go into music as a career. I graduated from UW-La Crosse with a degree in music education and trumpet performance. From there, I decided to give the performance route a go. I was accepted to the Chicago College of the Performing Arts at Roosevelt University where I earned a Master's degree in Orchestral Trumpet.

While in grad school, I began to dabble in HTML. Nothing serious, just a little something to do on the side when gigging was slow. I always had an interest in computers, but never seriously considered it as a career. Time passed and I found work as a private music teacher, freelance musician, and public school teacher. That whole time, something in the back of my mind kept telling me to make the jump into software development. I had been doing the same thing my entire life. Music music music music. So I took the jump.

I left my teaching job, and began taking software development classes online. I fell in love immediately. I saw in my learning a chance to start anew in an exciting and growing field. I liked the daily challenges, the problem solving, and the notion that with the right tools, I can build pretty much anything. I wake up every day excited to learn new concepts, build something no one has ever built before, or excecute an idea that I had the night before.
